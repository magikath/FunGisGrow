Setting up your mushroom logs does require a few tools. Here’s what you’ll need to collect:

  • A hand drill and a 19/64th inch drill bit ( has the most compact fit to help the spawn spread quickly)
  • A wooden or rubber mallet (a standard hammer will work, too)
  • Sealing wax (food grade parraffin or beeswax are both safe and healthy options)
  • Something to melt your wax with, like a crockpot or old pan (a clean, empty tin can will also work)
  • A wool dauber (always good to have an extra)


Insturctions:
  1. Drill a line of 1 inch holes into the log every 6-8 inches lengthwise. Make these lines every 3-4 inches around the circumference of the log. These lines should be offset from each other forming a diomond in the area between the drill holes. 
  2. Tap in the plugs with a mallet or hammer so they sit just below the bark.
  3. Using food grade wax, melt and dab the wax over the holes so that the plug is completely sealed in.
  4. Stand logs up or againt each other to minimize contact with the soil.
  5. Water logs 2-3 times a week to keep up moisture levels. 
  6. After the mycelium builds up in the logs you can connect them together to connect their transport networks.
  7. Wait for fruiting and enjoy your hard work! Fungi have evolved to create molecules no other organism can assemble. They will work magic on your health! I reccomend watching documentaries about fungi and their mushrooms, there is so much to learn.
